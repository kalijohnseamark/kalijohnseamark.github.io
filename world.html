The Foundation

The Source

All living things are connected through the Source. It flows through the world in a constant spiral — through people, through animals, through the oldest trees and the youngest shoots. Most never know it is there. A rare few learn to see it. Fewer still learn what seeing it truly means.

The Sharin

The inner pathway through which the Source moves in a person. Those who find stillness within themselves begin to sense it. Those who master it can do things that have no other name but impossible.

The Source will not bend to your will until you have mastery over yourself. — The Soothsayers of the Wood
Keletkazesh

The Faith of Ishwar

Before there was the world, there was a pantheon. And among that pantheon, none held more power than Ishwar — master of creation itself. He could raise mountains and carve rivers from the land. He fell in love with Sol, goddess of fertility and water, and she with him. The other gods could not bear it.

Led by Mani — god of the wind, and Sol's own twin brother — the pantheon conspired. Sol was sealed in a great ball of fire and cast into the sky, beyond Ishwar's reach. When Ishwar learned the truth, he unleashed his power upon himself and became a great whirlpool, absorbing the gods who came against him one by one — until Mani called a cyclone and scattered Ishwar's waters across the world forever. Ishwar was never the same again.

Ishwar

The Divine Wind  ·  Creator God

Scattered across the world as the wind itself. He is everywhere and nowhere. The Kershan believe that when he regains enough of himself, he will walk the earth once more.

Sol

The Sun  ·  Goddess of Fertility

Sealed in fire and cast into the sky. She searches for a way back to the earth and to Ishwar. Where her light falls, the land grows. Her warmth is not metaphor.

Mani

The Moon  ·  God of the Wind

He chases his sister across the sky in endless contrition. Where he passes, night falls. The Kershan do not wage war in darkness, nor hold celebrations in it.

The Koveto

Children of Ishwar  ·  The Faithful

Practitioners of Keletkazesh — the religion of Ishwar. Pilgrims, missionaries, and zealots in equal measure. They can be found in every corner of Estara.

All celebrations of the Kershan people are held during the day, where the light of Sol watches over the children of Ishwar. — Keletkazesh
The Chosen

The Deva

As Ishwar drifts across the world as the Divine Wind, he releases fragments of his power into the Kershan bloodline — and where that power finds purchase, a Deva is born. They are worshipped as the fingers of Ishwar's hand. Deified in life. And the price of being chosen is absolute: a Deva's children are born barren. The line ends with them.

Major Deva carry great elemental power, considered righteous and beyond question by the faithful. Minor Deva hold lesser or more unsettling gifts — mistrusted, yet still revered. Both serve a purpose. The Kershan have never had a Wind Deva. They await one with dread and hope in equal measure.

Major Deva

Fire Deva

Commands flame. Can cloak themselves in fire, absorb intense heat, and cast great sprays of it. Among the most feared in open battle.

Major Deva

Water Deva

Commands all moisture. High-pressure torrents, crashing waves, cutting steam. Limited only by what water is near — so they are never far from it.

Major Deva

Lightning Deva

Calls storms, channels electricity, casts shockwaves. Weather permitting, they can bring lightning from a clear sky.

Major Deva

Ice Deva

Conjures ice in any form — walls, spikes, beams. A sufficiently powerful Ice Deva can lower the temperature of an entire region.

Major Deva

Beast Deva

Imposes their will on any creature and commands them absolutely. Almost always accompanied by a familiar. The limits of what they can control are not yet fully understood.

Major Deva

Swarm Deva

Becomes the hive mind of any insect they choose — thousands at once. Among the most autonomously powerful of all Deva, and the most difficult to work alongside.

Minor Deva

Mirage Deva

Casts illusions targeting any of the five senses. What cannot harm you is merely the cover. What hides within the illusion is another matter entirely.

Minor Deva

Mimic Deva

Changes physical form. A novice takes another person's face. A master becomes any beast in Estara they have ever encountered.

Minor Deva

Desire Deva

Irresistible to all who lay eyes on them. A song, a dance, a glance — and a victim's will begins to bend. Few who fall under their spell find their way back unassisted.

Minor Deva

Bane Deva

Poisonous blood. Poisonous saliva. Immune to every toxin they have ever encountered. They can detect poison in food or drink at a glance.

Minor Deva

Renewal Deva

Regrows lost limbs. Heals themselves of almost anything. Does not age in any ordinary sense. Their one true vulnerability is known, and closely guarded.

Minor Deva

Healing Deva

Heals the wounds of others — broken bones, bleeding, sickness. Cannot regrow limbs. Cannot save a person who has already given up.

Minor Deva

Brawn Deva

Five to seven times stronger than any ordinary person. More intelligent than most would guess — and they resent being underestimated for it.

Minor Deva

Fleetfoot Deva

Faster than a horse at full gallop. Tremendous stamina. A terrifying knife fighter. Also possessed of an enormous appetite.

Minor Deva

Flight Deva

Takes to the sky. Used as scouts and builders alike. Known to shirk responsibility in favour of their own wandering. The sky, they will tell you, belongs to no one.

Minor Deva

Patthar Deva

Stone-like skin. Resistant to most elemental attacks. The vanguard of military campaigns. Formidably strong — and notably, almost comically, terrible swimmers.

The Prophecy of the Koveto

When a Deva of Wind walks the earth, Ishwar will have the power to return to flesh. The children of Ishwar will march on Tirtha. The world will know unending peace.

The Continent

The Lands of Estara

Kisirakar

The Kershan Empire · East

Harsh, arid, vast. A land of spice and silk where the sun is merciless and the people harder still. Only recently unified from warring kingdoms into a single empire — the largest nation on the continent. Its rulers wield elemental power. Its people are deeply, fervently faithful. They believe their god walks among them as the wind.

Otthon

The Hinterlands · North

Cold, densely forested, ancient. The oldest land on the continent. Strange creatures move in its deeper woods, and stranger things still are said to live at its heart. The Hinterfolk are tall, pale, and hard — shaped by a way of life older than any kingdom. Something in these woods holds the history of the world. It does not share it freely.

Karland

The True Kingdom · South

The first proper kingdom and still the heart of the continent. Its royal family has been absent for sixteen years. No one has filled the throne. The noble families hold the country together in their own fashion — which is to say, they argue over it constantly. Its capital, Tamlith, is home to the greatest institution of learning on the continent, and to secrets that certain people would kill to protect.

Sonnareth

The Sommerset Lands · West

A land of peace, rich coastal plains, and open trade. Sommerset feeds much of the continent. It has no standing army and makes no apologies for it. The Awari people are careful, prosperous, and neutral — a combination that has served them very well. How long it continues to do so is another question.

Tamlith, Karland

Wissen Shiki
College

Six hundred years old and still the most important building in Karland. Wissen Shiki stands in Tamlith at the edge of Lunar Bay — three great towers rising above the city skyline: the White Tower, the Black Tower, and between them the High Tower, climbing higher than anything else in sight. Inside: brass pipework and copper ceilings, steam-powered lights, boilers hidden behind the walls. A place of genuine wonder, and of carefully curated knowledge.

Students come from across the continent to study metallurgy, alchemy, mathematics, geology, electromagnetism, and a dozen other disciplines. Progress is measured in metals — ten levels, Bismuth to Gold. The path from first year to Journeyman Artificer takes a decade, following the traditional route. The brilliant, the impatient, and the brave find other ways.

The Challenge System

Prove You Know It

A student who believes they already hold the knowledge of the next metal level may challenge it — facing a peer who already holds that rank in a live demonstration of skill. Pass, and you advance. Fail, and you wait. A decade can become five years for those willing to work for it.

Critical Engagements

The Unlisted Class

No metal ranking. No formal syllabus. Required for the entire duration of a student's time at the college. It covers the application of knowledge under pressure — against opponents who are trying to stop you. It is taught by one professor, in one room, with no official record of what happens inside it.

The Noble Families

Old Blood, Old Debts

The Blacktowers, Whitetowers, and Hightowers helped build this college. They have never stopped profiting from it. The dean is a Blacktower. Half of Tamlith owes the Hightowers money. The Moonstones control trade. Behind all of it, a vacant throne that no one has been able to claim for sixteen years.

The Eastern Library

The No Access Zone

Most of the college is open to students. Most of it. There is a section of the eastern library that requires special authorisation. Students have been expelled for entering it. What the college does not want its students to find may be the most important thing in the building.

Creatures of Estara

Beasts of the World

Estara is not a tame world. Its creatures are old — some older than memory — and many carry a connection to the Source that no human practitioner has fully understood. A few are considered sacred. Most are simply dangerous. Some are both.

Szarvas

Hinterlands  ·  Singular

The great deer of the wood. A singular creature. Life is said to flourish wherever it passes. The Hinterfolk and the Vorsa protect it absolutely, and do not explain why.

Sarkany

Estara  ·  Two Kinds

Elemental dragons with the deepest natural Source connection of any living creature. And the great multi-headed breed — no Source, more heads, considerably more intelligent. The older ones speak.

Toll Wulf

Hinterlands  ·  Solitary

A great wolf that hunts alone. Far larger than any natural wolf. Connected to the Source through the Earth element. Its pelt is valuable for reasons beyond warmth.

Kurma

Eastern Coast  ·  Ancient

A great turtle living off the Hinterlands coast. Very old. Very patient. Pilgrims have traveled great distances to sit at its feet and ask their questions. It answers some of them.

Turul

Estara  ·  Rare

A giant eagle of legendary bearing. Sighting one is considered seven years of good fortune. They do not acknowledge this. They do not acknowledge men at all, generally.

Vorsa

Hinterlands  ·  Ancient Race

Spirits of the forest. Bark-skinned, moss-haired, peaceful — until the forest is threatened. One of the oldest races in the world. Possibly older than anything that walked upright before them.

Tengu

Mountains  ·  Neutral

Crow-headed humanoids. Masterful blacksmiths and sword fighters. They will not fight you without reason. They are fond of riddles. Give them a bad one at your own risk.

Yali

Kershan  ·  Apex

Tiger's body. Elephant's tusks. Serpentine scales. The most feared land predator in Kisirakar — which is saying a great deal, given the competition.

Shadhavar

Kershan  ·  Sacred

A one-horned creature whose perforated horn sings with the wind — joyous or melancholy depending on the direction of the breeze. Sacred. Also hunted. Its horn becomes an heirloom.

Gorebeast

Estara  ·  Prized

A mare with a curved horn — or two. The stallions have no horns and are far more docile. The females are connected to the Source through the Wind element, and their ivory horns are among the finest Source amplifiers known. They are considered prize game wherever they are found.

Wolpertinger

Estara  ·  Elusive

A rabbit with the wings of a bird and the antlers of a deer. Its feet are considered a lucky talisman — rare enough that most people selling rabbit feet as wolpertinger feet are lying, and know it.

Basan

Estara  ·  Sacred in some lands

A large bird of brilliant plumage that breathes ghostfire — flame that holds no heat and burns nothing. Considered sacred in certain regions. Garb made from its feathers carries a faint connection to the Source through the Flame element.

Kappa

Wetlands  ·  Hostile

A water creature with a turtle's shell and a bowl of water set into the top of its head. It lives in bogs, under bridges, and wherever shallow water gathers — and it tries to drown whatever it finds. The bowl is its only weakness. Spill it, and the matter is resolved.

Oni

Estara  ·  Dangerous

Great ogre-like creatures with horns and sharp teeth, found in small groups. Intelligent enough to speak and use the Source in rudimentary ways. They do not build, and they hold an intense and abiding malice toward humans.

Jorogumo

Estara  ·  Predatory

Giant spiders with the face of a beautiful woman on their back. The oldest and most powerful among them can speak, and temporarily take a woman's form. They hunt people exclusively when they can. The face, witnesses report, is always smiling.

Kitsune

Estara  ·  Clever

Shape-shifting foxes. They cannot become people, but can turn into flame, other animals, or inanimate objects. Small, clever, and generally well-liked by common folk. Some have been caught and kept as pets — which the kitsune invariably find more amusing than their owners do.

Nue

Estara  ·  Ill Omen

The head of a monkey on the body of a tiger, with some sporting wings. Encountering one is considered seven years of bad fortune — the precise inverse of the Turul. Its cry is horrible to hear. It would prefer to be left alone. It rarely is.

Manok

Estara  ·  Widespread

Goblins found across the whole continent, from the coldest reaches of the Hinterlands to the scorched south. Nuisances alone, genuinely dangerous in numbers. None of them ever grow larger than a ten-year-old child, which people tend to forget until it is too late.

Naga

Sommerset & Kershan  ·  Giant

Giant snakes, though the two varieties are quite different. The Kershan Naga resembles a great king cobra. The western Sommerset variety is built for both land and water, and looks more like something dredged up from the deep sea.

Raki

Helfort  ·  Venomous

Scorpions native to the scorched wastes, no larger than a dog and considerably more lethal. Extremely poisonous. Extremely hostile. Water is noxious to them — the one mercy the desert does not often provide.

Aleya

Kershan  ·  Ghostly

Pale blue orbs of light that drift through the desert wastes of Kisirakar, hovering just above the sand. They do not attack. They do not follow. They simply drift — and those who follow them too long are said to lose their way between the world of the living and whatever lies beyond it.

Vetala

Kershan  ·  Unnatural

Something is wrong with them before you see them. The cold reaches you first — then the smell. They move. They remember, after a fashion. But whatever they were in life has long since departed, and what remains wears the shape of it like borrowed clothes.
